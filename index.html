About me.

I hold a PhD in Psychology from the University of Bristol and have lived and worked in Taiwan, the UK, France, Belgium, and Germany. This multicultural journey shapes everything I do. Based in Berlin, I specialize in helping expats and international professionals navigate the emotional and behavioral challenges of living in new environments.

With a background in cognitive neuroscience, my approach blends evidence-based CBT with insights from how the brain learns, adapts, and heals. Combined with intercultural awareness, this creates a space where your cultural background, your neurobiology, and your current reality are all honoured.

CBT gives you practical, evidence-based tools to identify and change unhelpful thought patterns. I bring in insights from cognitive neuroscience to help you understand why your brain responds the way it does to stress, change, and cultural displacement. Combined with Intercultural Mindfulness, this creates an approach that addresses your inner world, your neurobiology, and the realities of living across cultures.

Sessions are 50 minutes, conducted via secure video. Online counselling offers the same depth as in-person work, with the flexibility of connecting from wherever you are. All you need is a private, quiet space and a stable connection.

My practice is designed for expats, international professionals, students, and anyone navigating life across cultures. Whether you’ve just arrived in a new country or have been abroad for years, if you’re dealing with burnout, career uncertainty, attachment challenges, or a sense of disconnection, this space is for you.
