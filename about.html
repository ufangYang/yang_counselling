From researcher to counsellor

I hold a Master's in Psychology from the University of Bristol and a PhD from France. I've lived and worked in Taiwan, the UK, France, Belgium, and Germany. That's five countries, four languages, and more moments of "who am I here?" than I can count.

This multicultural journey shapes everything I do. I know what it feels like to rebuild your sense of self in a new culture, to carry grief for a home that no longer fits, and to navigate professional life while translating between worlds.

My background in cognitive neuroscience means I don't just understand these challenges emotionally. I understand what's happening in the brain when you're stuck in burnout, when anxiety spikes before a meeting in your second language, or when you can't explain to anyone why moving abroad feels like loss.
